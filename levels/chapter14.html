In this chapter, you'll learn about lookaheads and lookbehinds in regex. These are zero-width assertions that allow you to match a pattern only if it is followed or preceded by another pattern, without including that pattern in the match. Lookaheads are denoted by (?=...) and (?!...), while lookbehinds are denoted by (?<=...) and (?<!...).
